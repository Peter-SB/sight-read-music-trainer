offline batch run (`pnpm test tests/noteSamples.test.ts`). Both runs agree.

## Findings

1. **Classifier accuracy is excellent across the tested range** (Ab2 up to D5,
   covering the tenor's low-to-mid register). Every sample resolved to the
   right note with 96–99% frame coverage and 99–100% agreement — no octave
   errors, no dropouts.
2. **Tuning error is small and unbiased**: cents deviation is -3 to +4 across
   all 12 notes, well inside a usable tuner's tolerance, and not skewed
   consistently sharp or flat.
3. **Two fixture-naming quirks found and fixed in the test harness** (not
   detector bugs — flagged here since they explain why raw scores looked bad
   before the fix):
   - Filenames encode the **written** (as-fingered) tenor note, but with an
     octave digit 2 lower than scientific pitch notation (`Bb1.mp3` is
     scientific `Bb3`). Ground truth now applies that offset plus the tenor's
     +14 semitone transposition before comparing to the detector's
     concert-pitch output.
   - A trailing `di` in a filename (`F3di`, `F2di`) is Italian for *diesis*
     ("sharp"), not junk — `F3di` means F#3. Confirmed because only reading it
     as a sharp made the detector's output line up.
4. **No samples below Ab2 or above D5 in this set** — the low end (concert
   Bb2/Ab2, ~104–117 Hz) and the extended/altissimo range are untested. Given
   the code's own comments flag low-register octave errors as the main risk
   area for McLeod pitch detection, that's the highest-value gap to fill with
   more samples.
5. **Sample durations are all ~2s** of a sustained tone — no data yet on
   articulation attacks, short notes, or dynamic/vibrato passages, which are
   more realistic sight-reading conditions than a held tone.

## Suggested next steps

- Add samples for the tenor's true extremes (low Bb/Ab and high altissimo) to
  stress-test the register the code comments call out as risky.
- Add short-note and vibrato samples to see how `coverage`/`agreement`
  degrade under more realistic playing conditions.
- No source code changes are needed based on this data — `noteMapper.ts` and
  `pitchDetector.ts` are performing well as-is.
